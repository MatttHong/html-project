Musical Career

Matthew Hong, 20, from Montvale, NJ, is currently pursuing a minor at the Blair School of Music , where he is studying under Daniel Reinker. Matthew started playing the violin at age 4, and in 2014, he was admitted to the Manhattan School of Music in the violin performance major under Dr. Maria Radicheva – pupil of Dorothy Delay. Though he thoroughly loves to play the violin, he fell in love with the rich tone of the viola and was readmitted as a viola performance major in 2016 under the tutelage of Karen Ritscher – pupil of Karen Tuttle. Throughout his musical career, Matthew has performed in venues such as Weill Recital Hall (NY), Riverside Church (NY), and the Bruno Walter Auditorium (NY). He has also received first place in competitions including the AFAF Golden Era of Romantic Music International Competition, the New York International Music Concours, the National Young Maestro Competition, and the American Protege International Piano and Strings Competition. In June 2018 and June 2019, Matthew studied with Michelle LaCourse and Hye Min Choi at the Boston University Tanglewood Institute to improve his craft as a musician.
